Bio

Just Call Me Wuj

Not many people believe me when I tell them that a few years ago I was semi-truck driver in Walla Walla, Washington (the town so nice, they named it twice). My path to design was a bit unconventional, that started out with me starting a T-shirt company with one of my best friends in 2010. My interests shifted from science to screen printing, to entrepreneurship, and eventually to trial-and-error “design” work. I developed my craft at University of San Francisco and received my Bachelor of Arts in 2014. Since graduating, I continue to explore a variety of opportunities in the design field. I have been a contractor and a freelance designer for the past two years with extensive experience in digital media with special interest in product design. I’ve worked with small business owners, non-profits, start-ups, universities and multinational corporations. I enjoy working on interdisciplinary teams and learning new skills and processes. I take pride in my work, but I also understand the bigger picture: teamwork makes the dream work.

Outside of the office, I love to stay active and enjoy what the great state of California has to offer. If it’s not baseball season when bleeding black and orange, I’m looking for the next set of waves in the water or finding a new trail outside of the city to explore. On the weekends, I try to get away from the screen and make things with my hands. I’m currently building my garage woodshop piece-by-piece and I’m always on the lookout for the next used shop tool on Craigslist.

I’m currently searching for new exciting opportunities in the digital product space where I can learn, share, and create with the company. I’m always available to grab coffee and talk shop, Giants, or El Niño. Thanks for stopping by : - )
